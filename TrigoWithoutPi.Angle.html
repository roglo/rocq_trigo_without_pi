Angles Without π

In this approach, an angle is not a real number but a pair of real numbers (x, y) such that x² + y² = 1. The cosine is then x, and the sine is y.
The identity sin² + cos² = 1 holds by definition. We can define angle addition (see below), and the usual formulas for cos(x + y) and sin(x + y) follow naturally.
There is no need for the number π here; the angle π is simply (-1, 0).
